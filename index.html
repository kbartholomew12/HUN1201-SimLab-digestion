Start Your Case

Purpose

Use digestive-system evidence to explain why some swallowed substances can work inside the GI tract while others must survive digestion and reach the bloodstream intact. This will help you evaluate health claims and understand how digestion, absorption, and medication delivery are connected.

Task

Complete one randomly assigned case involving a hormone, enzyme, medication, or absorption problem. At five checkpoints, analyze what happens to the substance and decide whether the case claim is supported, partially supported, or not supported.

Instructions

  1. Enter your name and select Start Random Case.
  2. Read the claim and identify what must happen for the substance to work.
  3. Complete all five checkpoints. Checkpoints 1–4 are worth 2 points each. Checkpoint 5 is worth 4 points total: 2 points for the verdict and 2 points for selecting the strongest evidence. Use the Evidence Panel to follow molecule integrity, site of action, absorption potential, local GI effects, and possible nutrient impacts.
  4. After the verdict, review your case report and answer the two open-ended questions in your own words.
  5. Save the finished report as a PDF on your desktop and upload the PDF to the Canvas assignment.
Key question: Must this substance survive digestion, act inside the GI tract, or be broken down as part of digestion?

Successful work uses the assigned case evidence, distinguishes digestion from absorption, and explains the process clearly rather than only repeating definitions.

Student Information

Evidence Panel

How to Read the Evidence

Molecule integrity: Is the substance still intact, partly fragmented, or fully broken down?

Site of action: Where does it need to work: GI lumen, intestinal wall, bloodstream, or target tissue?

Absorption potential: Is it ready to cross the intestinal border?

Local GI effect: What is changing in the digestive environment?

Secondary nutrient impact: Does this case also affect digestion or absorption of other nutrients?
